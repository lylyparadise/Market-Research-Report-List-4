<p><strong>The Fluorescent Chloride Sensor Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Quinoline-Based Chloride</li><li>Forster Resonance Energy Transfer Based Chloride</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Quinoline-based chloride sensors are fluorescent molecules that change their emission properties in the presence of chloride ions, making them suitable for detecting chloride levels in biological and environmental samples. On the other hand, Forster Resonance Energy Transfer (FRET) based chloride sensors work by utilizing the energy transfer between two fluorescent molecules in the presence of chloride ions. Both types of sensors are commonly used in research and industrial applications for monitoring chloride levels due to their high sensitivity and selectivity.</p></p>
