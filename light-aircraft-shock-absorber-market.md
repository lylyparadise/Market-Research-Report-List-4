<p><p>Light Aircraft Shock Absorbers are classified into mechanical and hydraulic types based on their operation mechanism. Mechanical shock absorbers use springs and metal plates to absorb and dissipate energy generated during landing and takeoff. On the other hand, hydraulic shock absorbers utilize hydraulic fluid and valves to provide a smoother and more controlled dampening effect. Both types of shock absorbers play a crucial role in ensuring safe and comfortable landings for light aircraft by reducing impact forces and vibrations.</p></p>
