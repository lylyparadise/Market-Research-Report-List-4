<p><ul><li>Low Temperature Freezer</li><li>Ultra Low Temperature Freezer</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Laboratory freezers are classified into three main types based on their temperature ranges. Low temperature freezers typically operate between -20°C to -40°C and are commonly used for storing enzymes, reagents, and other biological samples. Ultra low temperature freezers operate at temperatures below -40°C, typically ranging from -40°C to -86°C, and are ideal for long-term storage of fragile samples like proteins and vaccines. Other types of laboratory freezers include cryogenic freezers, explosion-proof freezers, and plasma freezers, each catering to specific research and storage needs.</p></p>
