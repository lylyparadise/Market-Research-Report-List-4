<p><p>Organic surfactants are categorized into three main types based on their chemical properties and behavior in solutions: anionic, cationic, and nonionic. Anionic surfactants are negatively charged and commonly used in household cleaners and personal care products. Cationic surfactants are positively charged and are often found in hair care products and fabric softeners. Nonionic surfactants have no charge and are versatile in their applications, including in agriculture and pharmaceuticals. Each type of surfactant offers unique benefits and uses in various industries.</p></p>
