<p><p>Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) come in two main types: semi-automated and fully automated. Semi-automated AEDs require the user to manually press a button to deliver a shock to the patient, while fully automated AEDs deliver the shock automatically without any user intervention. Both types are designed to quickly diagnose and treat sudden cardiac arrest, ensuring that life-saving treatment can be administered promptly in emergency situations. These devices are crucial in public places, healthcare settings, and homes for effectively restoring normal heart rhythm.</p></p>
