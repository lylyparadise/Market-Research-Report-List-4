<p><p>Common-rail injection EFI engines come in two main types: SDI (Solenoid Direct Injection) and TDI (Turbo Direct Injection) markets. SDI engines use solenoid valves to control the fuel injection process, while TDI engines incorporate a turbocharger to improve engine performance. Both types offer high fuel efficiency, reduced emissions, and improved power output compared to traditional fuel injection systems. These engines are becoming increasingly popular in the automotive industry due to their superior performance and environmental benefits.</p></p>
