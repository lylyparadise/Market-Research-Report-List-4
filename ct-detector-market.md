<p><p>A single detector CT scanner uses only one detector to capture images of the body during a scan, resulting in slower image acquisition. In contrast, a multi-detector CT scanner utilizes multiple detectors to simultaneously capture images, allowing for quicker image acquisition and improved image quality. Multi-detector CT scanners are capable of producing higher resolution images in a shorter amount of time compared to single detector scanners, making them more efficient and preferred in many medical settings.</p></p>
