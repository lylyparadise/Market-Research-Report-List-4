<p><p>Mycotoxin binders are natural or synthetic substances used to prevent the harmful effects of mycotoxins in livestock feed. Raw clay binders, such as bentonite, are widely used due to their high binding capacity. Zeolites and other treated aluminosilicates have specific structures that can effectively bind mycotoxins. Polysaccharides, like yeast cell walls, are also commonly used binders. Other types of binders may include activated carbon or synthetic polymers. Each type of binder has unique properties that make them suitable for different mycotoxins and feed formulations.</p></p>
